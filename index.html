Our Philosophy

What We Believe

At KidHub we firmly believe that building healthy relationships with children requires a foundation of mutual trust and respect. One facet of achieving this is ensuring that the children genuinely enjoy, and are excited to participate in, the activities that are being offered. We do this by catering the daily activities of the program to the interests of the children that attend, and while the children will always be encouraged to participate, they will never be forced. We will always offer free play as an option during our planned activities so that children can join in at their own pace when they wish.

We are committed to helping build each child's self-esteem and self-worth by providing a welcoming and warm environment where children are free to learn new things as they develop their uniqueness and reach their maximum potential. We believe that integrating leadership-enhancing opportunities, connecting to the community and preparing for the future (among many other initiatives that we are involved in at KidHub) into our program is what sets us apart from other programs. We do this through many avenues such as KidHub Holiday Committees, KidHub Economy (which includes the Bank of KidHub and the KidHub Store), KidHub Cares, KidHub Clubs, and much more! Children will be encouraged to get involved in developing ideas for activities to help build their independence, creativity, personal responsibility, teamwork and problem-solving skills.

At KidHub we believe that it is essential for children to be provided with an atmosphere where their physical, social, lingual, intellectual, creative and emotional needs are taken seriously and that every effort is made to meet the needs of each child that attends the program. We understand that each child is unique in the way in which their needs are met and we believe it is our duty as child care providers to do what we can to best meet their needs in these areas. Our program will promote differentiated learning through a variety of activities (indoor/outdoor, small group, large group, individual, etc.) that will account for the uniqueness of the children who attend.

About the Owners

Who We Are

Rachel & Luke Docksteader were both raised in beautiful Prince Edward Island. Both being driven & business-minded individuals, they decided to combine Rachel’s passion for working with elementary school-aged children and Luke’s years of training and experience in software engineering to form an exciting school-age program that uses the most up-to-date technology — and KidHub was born in August 2015!

Rachel has a Bachelor of Education (UNB) and a Bachelor of Arts with a double-major in History and Psychology (UPEI). Luke has a Masters of Science in Electrical and Computer Engineering (Lakehead University) as well as a Bachelor of Technology in Electronics Engineering Technology (RCC Institute of Technology).

Rachel & Luke have three amazing children and love spending their time going on family outings, playing board games with friends and family, and spending as much time on the beach in the summer as possible!
